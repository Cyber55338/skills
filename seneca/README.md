## The 8 Seneca Frameworks

### 1. **Premeditatio Malorum** (Negative Visualization)
**What it is**: Visualizing worst-case scenarios to build resilience and gratitude

**When to use**: Before major events, when anxious, to cultivate gratitude

**Quick practice**:
- List 3 worst-case scenarios
- For each: How would I prevent it? How would I recover if it happened?
- Realize: Most fears are survivable

**Key quote**: *"We suffer more often in imagination than in reality."*

---

### 2. **Dichotomy of Control**
**What it is**: Separating what you can and cannot control

**When to use**: Whenever feeling frustrated, anxious, or helpless

**Quick practice**:
- Ask: "Is this within my control?"
- If NO â†’ Accept it, redirect energy
- If YES â†’ Take action
- If PARTIAL â†’ Control your effort, accept outcome uncertainty

**Key quote**: *"We cannot choose our external circumstances, but we can always choose how we respond."*

---

### 3. **Memento Mori** (Remember Death)
**What it is**: Contemplating mortality to clarify priorities and live intentionally

**When to use**: When procrastinating, wasting time, or feeling scattered

**Quick practice**:
- Morning: "You may not sleep again tonight"
- Evening: "You may not wake up tomorrow"
- Ask: "If I had 1 year left, would this matter?"

**Key quote**: *"Begin at once to live, and count each separate day as a separate life."*

---

### 4. **Voluntary Discomfort**
**What it is**: Deliberately practicing hardship to build resilience and reduce dependence on comfort

**When to use**: To build mental toughness, overcome fear of loss, regular training

**Quick practice**:
- Try: Cold shower, skip a meal, sleep on floor, walk in uncomfortable weather
- Ask during: "Is this what I feared?"
- Realize: I'm more resilient than I thought

**Key quote**: *"It is in times of security that the spirit should be preparing itself for difficult times."*

---

### 5. **Time as Precious Resource**
**What it is**: Managing your finite time intentionally

**When to use**: When overwhelmed, living for others, procrastinating

**Quick practice**:
- Evening: "Did I own my time today, or did others?"
- Filter: "If I had 1 year left, would this make the list?"
- Eliminate ruthlessly

**Key quote**: *"It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste a lot of it."*

---

### 6. **Evening Reflection**
**What it is**: Daily self-examination to track progress and learn from mistakes

**When to use**: Every evening (5-15 minutes)

**Quick practice**:
- What bad habit did I cure today?
- What virtue did I practice?
- What could I have done better?
- Tomorrow's intention: ___

**Key quote**: *"I examine my entire day and go back over what I've done and said, hiding nothing from myself."*

---

### 7. **Virtue Framework**
**What it is**: Making decisions based on character (wisdom, justice, courage, temperance) rather than outcomes

**When to use**: Ethical dilemmas, difficult decisions, seeking meaning

**Quick practice**:
- Identify the 4 virtues at play:
  - **Wisdom**: What's the wise choice?
  - **Justice**: What's fair?
  - **Courage**: What's brave?
  - **Temperance**: What's moderate?
- Choose virtue over outcome
- Accept consequences

**Key quote**: *"Virtue itself is its own reward."*

---

### 8. **Amor Fati** (Love Your Fate)
**What it is**: Accepting and embracing everything that happens; working with reality rather than against it

**When to use**: After setbacks, when fighting reality, stuck in resentment

**Quick practice**:
1. Feel the emotion (don't suppress)
2. Accept: "This happened. I can't change it."
3. Ask: "What's in my control now?"
4. Find the gift: "What can I learn? What opportunity does this create?"
5. Move forward

**Key quote**: *"Fate leads the willing, and drags along the reluctant."*

## Core Stoic Principles (Summary)

1. **Focus on what you control** (your thoughts, actions, character)
2. **Accept what you can't control** (others, outcomes, past, much of future)
3. **Live as if each day is a complete life** (don't postpone living)
4. **Prepare for adversity in peacetime** (train before you need it)
5. **Choose virtue over outcomes** (character is the only true success)
6. **Examine yourself daily** (continuous self-improvement)
7. **Practice discomfort regularly** (build resilience)
8. **Work with reality, not against it** (amor fati)
